## Vedic Astrology is Inherently Mathematical

The greatest misconception about astrology is that it relies purely on "intuition" or "magic." In reality, Jyotisha is a highly sophisticated branch of applied mathematics and astronomy. 

The ancient sages (Rishis) were phenomenal astronomers. To cast a chart, they had to calculate:
1.  **Planetary Longitudes:** The exact degree and minute of planets moving across the 360-degree zodiac.
2.  **Ephemeris Alignment:** Adjusting for the retrograde (backward) motion of planets.
3.  **Ayanamsha:** Calculating the precession of the Earth's equinox—the slight wobble of the Earth on its axis that shifts the backdrop of the stars over centuries.

## The Flaw of Human Calculation

While the ancient formulas were perfect, human execution often fell short. 

Even today, village astrologers using manual *Vakiya* (traditional) Panchangams frequently make slight mathematical rounding errors. In astrology, a calculation error of just three degrees can shift your entire Moon Sign (Rasi) or Birth Star (Nakshatra). An error of just a few minutes in determining the Ascendant (Lagna) can completely change the 12 houses of your chart.

When the foundation (the math) is flawed, the entire prediction collapses.

Vedic astrology uses a 120-year timeline system called the Vimshottari Dasha.
