# The Significance of Sashti Vratam: Conquering the 6 Inner Demons

In the Hindu spiritual tradition, fasting is rarely just about abstaining from food. It is a calculated bio-energetic practice designed to redirect physical energy into spiritual awakening. Among the most powerful of these fasting days is **Sashti**, the sixth lunar phase (Tithi) of the fortnight. 

Revered widely across Tamil Nadu and among global devotees of **Lord Murugan** (also known as Kartikeya, Skanda, or Subrahmanya), observing a *Vratam* (vow of fasting) on Sashti is believed to bring profound worldly victories and unparalleled spiritual strength.

But why is the number six so critical? And what exactly are we fighting when we observe Sashti Vratam?

## The Mythology: The Battle of Soorasamharam

The significance of Sashti is immortally tied to the legend of Lord Murugan, the commander-in-chief of the celestial army. 

According to the Skanda Purana, the heavens were captured by a terrifying trio of asuras (demons): Surapadman, Tarakasura, and Singamukha. These demons had secured boons that made them nearly invincible, spreading chaos across the universe. In response to the pleas of the Devatas, Lord Shiva manifested six sparks of fire from his third eye, which eventually formed the six-faced infant, Lord Murugan.

Armed with the *Vel* (the divine spear representing supreme wisdom and focus) given to him by Goddess Parvati, Lord Murugan waged a fierce six-day war against Surapadman and his massive army. 

On the sixth day of the waxing moon (Shukla Paksha Sashti) in the Tamil month of Aippasi, Murugan ultimately defeated Surapadman. Rather than killing him, Murugan split the demon in two, transforming one half into a peacock (his divine mount) and the other half into a rooster (the emblem on his flag). This monumental victory is celebrated annually as **Skanda Sashti** or *Soorasamharam*.

## The Spiritual Mechanics: The Six Inner Demons

Vedic myths are rarely just historical battles; they are profound psychological metaphors. Supreme wisdom dictates that the demons we face are not external entities, but internal flaws. 

Surapadman and his brothers represent the immense power of the human ego. Similarly, human consciousness is constantly besieged by six classic internal enemies, known as the *Arishadvargas*:
1.  **Kama** (Lust / Unbridled Desire)
2.  **Krodha** (Anger)
3.  **Lobha** (Greed)
4.  **Moha** (Delusion / Attachment)
5.  **Mada** (Arrogance / Pride)
6.  **Matsarya** (Jealousy)

Lord Murugan, with his six faces (Shanmukha), represents the divine wisdom required to conquer these six internal foes. 

When a devotee observes **Sashti Vratam**, they are essentially simulating this internal war. By denying the physical body its basic craving (food), the practitioner starves the ego. The empty stomach shifts the body’s energy from digestion to internal healing and spiritual concentration. 

## How to Observe Sashti Vratam

While the grand Skanda Sashti in the month of Aippasi is a 6-day severe penance, devout followers observe a simple fast on the Sashti Tithi of *every* lunar month, especially during the Shukla Paksha (waxing moon).

*   **The Fasting Rule**: The fast runs from sunrise to sunset, or for the precise duration of the Sashti Tithi. Some prefer strict water fasting, while others consume milk and fruits. The key is *Sattvic* restriction.
*   **The Practice**: The day is spent reciting the *Kanda Sashti Kavacham* (a powerful armor of hymns) or chanting the mantra `"Om Saravanabhava"`. 
*   **The Breaking**: The fast is broken only after visiting a Murugan temple (or praying at home) and viewing the moon or stars, depending on local family traditions.

## The Blessings of Sashti

Why do millions of people observe this fast every month? The energetic vibrations of the 6th lunar day, combined with the austerity of the fast, invoke fierce Martian energy (Mars is associated with Murugan).

1.  **Granting Progeny**: Astrologically, the 5th and 6th houses are crucial. Sashti Vratam is universally renowned as a powerful remedy for couples struggling with fertility issues, blessing them with healthy children.
2.  **Overcoming Debts and Enemies**: Just as Murugan destroyed the Asuras, this fast grants the practitioner the mental fortitude and circumstantial luck to conquer financial debts and suppress hidden enemies or legal troubles.
3.  **Sharpening Focus**: The *Vel* (spear) represents a sharp, unyielding intellect. Fasting on Sashti clears brain fog and grants students and professionals incredible focus and determination.

## Conclusion

Sashti Vratam is the ultimate spiritual weapon for the modern warrior. We may not be fighting mythical demons, but we battle anxiety, greed, debt, and distraction every single day. By fasting on the 6th lunar day, we invoke the supreme commander's energy, transforming our internal chaos into focused, victorious peace.
